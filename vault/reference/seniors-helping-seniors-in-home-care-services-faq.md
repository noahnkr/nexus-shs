Seniors Helping Seniors® Clients

What makes Seniors Helping Seniors® in-home care services unique?

Our Mission
The mission of Seniors Helping Seniors® in-home care services is simple:

To provide our seniors with the ability to choose an independent lifestyle
in their own homes, for as long as possible, with the dignity and respect
they deserve by finding caring seniors to help each other.

Our Goal

The goal of Seniors Helping Seniors® in-home care services is to match active seniors who can provide a

service in our community to other seniors who would benefit from receiving those services. We do our best

to match personalities and activity levels and to encourage a strong friendship that is developed as seniors

complete various activities throughout the day together. Like getting a little help from your friends®.

Seniors Helping Seniors® in-home care services reflects a clear understanding of seniors’ needs and what

we can do to make their lives easier. That understanding starts with our caregivers—friendly,

compassionate seniors who know the challenges that come with aging. Every day, our caregivers

brighten the lives of fellow seniors.

Our senior care services offer much more than help with day-to-day tasks. We take the time to  get to

know our caregivers; through careful interview, checks, and evaluations, our agency finds uncommonly

wonderful and reliable seniors to help you. Before matching you with one of our seniors, we take the

time to get to know you. As a result, we match seniors who are bound to get along, and we’ve helped

create many new friendships. We invite you to learn more about the in-home services that we provide.

No matter which you need, you’ll discover that a warm, caring, and dedicated senior caregiver can make

all the difference in the world.

What in-home care services do you provide?
We provide the following services to our seniors in their homes, assisted living, or nursing home facilities.

* Not all services available in all locations

Companionship
Our caregivers enjoy friendly conversation and companionship when providing care for a

fellow senior, as much as the senior receiving care does. Regular interactions with a kind,

friendly caregiver can help lift a senior’s spirits and keep them mentally active and engaged.

Personal Care Assistance
Our senior care caregivers are trained in personal care and daily living assistance, meaning

that they provide help with eating and drinking, bathing, and showering, toileting, dressing,

and mobility assistance. Our caregivers take pride in delivering care in a manner that they

themselves would desire from a caregiver.

Dementia & Alzheimer’s Care
Caring for a loved one with memory loss, dementia or Alzheimer’s disease can be very

challenging and  can result in burnout of even the most devoted family member over time.

Our caregivers are trained in how to recognize the distinct stages of these conditions,

manage difficult behaviors, and keep family members and healthcare caregivers up to date

on the senior’s condition.

Cooking & Shopping
If you’re only in need of a little help, you and your caregiver can prepare meals as a team, or

go to the grocery store together. If you’re in need of more assistance, your caregiver can go

to the grocery store and cook meals for you on their own.

Light Housekeeping
Our caregivers can assist with light housekeeping services that include cleaning kitchens,

dusting, vacuuming, sweeping, mopping, cleaning bathrooms, taking out the trash and

recycling, cleaning interior windows that can be reached without a ladder, organizing

closets and drawers, and general tidying around the home.

Escort to Appointments & Events
Getting out to visit with friends, attend events, or keep appointments is great for a senior’s

mental, emotional, and physical health. When a senior is unable to make trips on their own,

our caregivers can provide transportation to things like doctor visits, beauty salon or

barbershop appointments, social functions, family gatherings, etc.

Pet Care
Pets can be extremely important to a senior’s mental, emotional, and physical health.

Nevertheless, caring for a pet can become more challenging with age. Our caregivers can

help ensure that pets are getting the attention they need to stay healthy and happy; by

feeding and grooming pets, and taking dogs on walks.

Running Errands
In  addition  to  grocery  shopping,  our  caregivers  can  run  a  variety  of  errands  including

dropping  off  mail  at  the  post  office,  getting  prescriptions  and  other  necessities  at  the

pharmacy, picking up dry cleaning, shopping for pet food, and more. You’re always welcome

to ride along if you’d enjoy an easy, enjoyable trip around town!

Safety & Fall Prevention
Falls can be profoundly serious for seniors. Our caregivers look for potential tripping hazards

and other injury risks when they visit a senior’s home, and can make corrections or

recommend that a service caregiver is contacted.

Outings & Trips
If you are wanting to get out and about for any reason, our caregivers can provide

transportation to and from a destination. We can take a trip to a park, catch a movie, enjoy

a concert, or even just go on a nice, casual cruise around town.

Light Handyman Services
Our in-home senior care services can include the tackling of simple handyman projects and

minor repairs. If a particular service is beyond what our caregiver can handle, they may be

able to give some insight on what’s involved and the type of service person to contact.

Yard Work
While fresh air and sunlight can do wonders for a senior’s outlook, too much yard work can

become taxing. Our caregivers can assist with mowing, weeding, and other outdoor

projects. Seniors can participate to whatever degree they are interested and able.

Long-Distance Check-Ins

For family members who live a long distance from their loved one and are unable to visit

them often, our caregivers can connect with them on a regular basis to provide updates on a

senior’s physical health, cognitive condition, and overall wellness.

Overnight Stays and 24-Hour Care

Our in-home senior care services are available every day of the week, and every hour of the

day, even overnight. That includes 24-hour care, if needed.

Mailing Letter & Bills
Staying on top of correspondence becomes more challenging as we age. Our caregivers

can help get cards, letters, and bills in the mail as requested by a senior.

How do I start services?
Call us to set up an appointment or reach out to us by visiting seniorshelpingseniors.com. We will come to

you and discuss how best we can help you. You decide what days and hours you would like.

Do I need to sign a contract for a specific amount of time?
No, we have a Seniors Helping Seniors® client agreement, so that we are all aware of the different costs

and services that are provided. We will go over it with you before signing and starting services.

When do I schedule services?
You can call us anytime to change your service schedule. If you are requesting a ride, please give us

24-hour notice to be able to schedule a caregiver for you.

Who provides my services?
All of our Seniors Helping Seniors® caregivers are typically seniors who love being around other people

and want to help others.  We try to have the same caregivers work with you each week.

Do you provide services in the evenings or weekends?
Yes. Our in-home care services are available every day of the week, and every hour of the day, even

overnight. That includes 24-hour care if needed.

Do I need to feed or give money to the caregiver?
No. Our Seniors Helping Seniors® caregivers are trained that they do not eat your food. They can eat a
meal they packed for themselves with you, but not to take your meal. Money should not be given to a
caregiver—payments should be made directly to your local Seniors Helping Seniors® office. Caregivers
also cannot accept tips, they are paid through Seniors Helping Seniors® in-home care services only.

Do you have a minimum of hours per visit?
Yes, minimums vary by location.

What if I want to discontinue services?
Cancellation varies by location.
